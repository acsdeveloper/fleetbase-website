Operational Dashboard

The operational dashboard is your control centre. On load, you see a clean, intuitive layout built around your role. At the top sits a row of real-time KPI cards showing trips today (count and status breakdown), available drivers (count and utilisation %), fleet health (vehicles in use, available, maintenance), and compliance score. Below that is an alert zone, sorted by severity: critical issues in red (action required now), warnings in orange (address within hours), and info items in blue (FYI). Each alert links directly to the data you need to fix it, so you're never hunting through menus. The main content area is a fully customisable widget grid. Dispatcher working a shift? Add "unassigned trips", "available drivers", and "vehicle status" widgets. Manager reviewing performance? Add "cost this week", "compliance violations", and "driver hours". Finance controller? Add "revenue recognised", "expenses pending approval", and "fuel cost trend". You drag widgets around, resize them, and save your layout. Week-over-week trip volume, cost trends, and compliance trends appear as simple charts that let you swipe between periods to spot patterns. Every KPI and alert is clickable. Click any alert or KPI number, and you instantly see the underlying data—which trips are unassigned, which drivers are available, which vehicles are down for maintenance. No extra clicks, no "go to another screen and search"—you're there. On mobile, the same KPIs and alerts are optimized for small screens, and critical alerts push notifications to your phone so you're never out of the loop. The layout adapts to your role automatically; different users see different priorities and different dashboards.

Why Choose Operational Dashboard

The operational dashboard is where intelligence meets action. Most fleet managers rely on fragmented reports, manual checks, and reactive responses to problems. By the time you notice an issue in a spreadsheet, revenue is already lost—a trip sits unassigned, a driver goes home, a vehicle breaks down.

FleetYes's operational dashboard prevents these invisible losses by making the entire operation visible in real-time. You don't wait for end-of-day summaries; you see unassigned trips in seconds. You know instantly when a driver hits their hours limit. You spot maintenance overdue before it triggers downtime.

The difference is immediate: dispatchers allocate trips faster, managers catch problems before they cascade, and the whole team moves from reactive to proactive. One dashboard replaces the spreadsheet chaos, the email chains, and the "why didn't anyone tell me?" conversations that cost thousands in lost efficiency.
