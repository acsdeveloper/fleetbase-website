Inventory Management

Inventory management transforms spare parts from a mystery to a managed asset with complete visibility and control. The inventory dashboard sits at the top with KPI cards showing total inventory value, number of items below threshold, stock-outs this month, and excess inventory that's tying up cash. Real-time stock level trends show how your inventory is moving. Below that is a parts inventory grid listing all parts with current stock level, reorder threshold, reorder quantity, and associated supplier. Colour-coding is instant: green means adequate stock, yellow means running low, red means out of stock. Sort by stock level, category, or supplier to find what you need. Click any part to open its detail card, showing stock level broken down by depot, complete usage history (when used, on which vehicle, by which technician), supplier and cost information, and reorder settings. Photos of the part are included so you know exactly what part is being tracked. Stock level alerts work automatically: a red alert fires when stock falls below threshold, a yellow warning when approaching threshold, and the system triggers a purchase order automatically to your approved supplier. When a technician installs a part during vehicle repair, they log usage directly in the system (which part, which vehicle, date, technician name), and the cost is automatically allocated to that vehicle's maintenance record. When stock falls below threshold, the system generates a purchase order and sends it to the supplier, showing reorder quantity, delivery address, and required delivery date. It can be approved manually or triggered automatically. If one depot is low on a part but another depot has excess, a technician can request a transfer. The manager approves if it maintains adequate stock at both locations, and levels adjust automatically. A supplier management section lists all approved suppliers for each part type, with contact info, lead times, and pricing tracked. The system can select the best supplier automatically based on cost and delivery time. On mobile, technicians search the parts inventory, log usage, and submit stock requests from the workshop or roadside, and the app works offline for areas with poor connectivity, syncing when a connection is available.

Large fleet maintenance operation

200+ vehicles, 3 depots, dozens of spare part types. Centralised inventory prevents shortages and double-ordering.

Preventive maintenance planning

Oil changes scheduled. Check inventory. Parts available? Schedule maintenance. Not available? Place order and schedule 2 weeks later.

Emergency repair management

Vehicle breaks down. Check inventory for common parts. In stock? Repair immediately. Not in stock? Order and schedule when available.

Cost control and negotiation

Multi-site operations

Head office manages inventory policy. Each franchise manages its own stock at branch level. HQ sees network-wide summary.

Why Choose Inventory Management

Inventory chaos costs thousands in excess stock, missed repairs, and emergency procurement. Without visibility, you over-stock expensive parts that sit unused, run out of common items mid-repair, order the same parts twice, and pay premium prices for emergency procurement.

FleetYes's inventory management puts control and visibility into your technician's hands. Stock levels are tracked in real-time. Reorder thresholds trigger automatic purchase orders. Technicians log part usage immediately, so costs are allocated correctly. Historical data shows what you actually use, so procurement becomes data-driven instead of guesswork.

The result is immediate: 30% reduction in holding costs, 40% faster repairs, zero stock-outs, and accurate cost allocation. Maintenance becomes a managed, predictable cost center instead of a source of surprises.
