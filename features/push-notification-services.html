How It Works

1

Event Triggers

An event occurs on your backend (new order assigned, message received, alert issued).

2

Send to Device

The backend sends a notification payload to Apple Push Notification service (iOS) or Firebase Cloud Messaging (Android).

3

Deliver to Driver

The platform's push service immediately routes the notification to the driver's device.

4

Driver Responds

Driver sees the notification on their lock screen or notification centre. They can respond directly or tap to open the app.

Why Choose Push Notification Services

Push notifications are how modern mobile apps keep users informed without requiring constant polling. Without them, drivers would have to open the app to see new orders-defeating the purpose of mobile delivery.

The platform-native approach (APNs for iOS, FCM for Android) ensures maximum reliability. Apple and Google handle delivery, retry, and reliability. Your app just needs to send the notification payload.

Rich notifications are particularly powerful. Instead of a bare "New Order," you can send a notification with the customer name, address, and order amount visible immediately. Drivers decide whether to open the app based on the preview. Some notifications can have actionable buttons for immediate responses without opening the app.
