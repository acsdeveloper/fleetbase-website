## How It Works

1. **Driver checks vehicle before accepting trip** — Opens mobile app. Walks around vehicle and takes photos. Notes any damage or mechanical issues.

2. **System stores inspection data** — Photos geotagged with location and timestamp. Damage severity flagged (minor, major, needs repair).

3. **Manager reviews for issues** — Dashboard shows flagged damage. Critical issues trigger alerts. Service team notified to address before next trip.

4. **Post-trip inspection confirms no new damage** — Driver repeats inspection after trip. Comparison shows what changed during delivery. Disputes resolved with photo evidence.

## Visual Overview

Vehicle inspection is a simple, visual process built directly into the mobile app for every trip. When a driver starts their day, they tap "Start Inspection" and the app displays a quick inspection checklist (tyres, lights, mirrors, obvious damage). The driver taps "Take Photo" to add photos, then swipes through sections of the vehicle (front, sides, back, interior, cargo area), photographing each area. They can add written notes to specific photos (e.g., "small dent on nearside wing"). When finished, they tap "Complete" and the inspection is timestamped and geotagged.

If the driver identifies damage, they can flag it directly in the app by drawing an annotation on the photo (circling a dent, for example), describing the damage, and rating its severity (minor, significant, safety-critical). After the trip completes, the driver runs a post-trip inspection using the same process—new photos of the vehicle. The system automatically compares pre-trip and post-trip images to identify new damage and flags obvious changes. An inspection history card on the trip detail page shows pre-trip and post-trip photos side-by-side, with a timeline showing when each photo was taken and where (geotagged location data included).

A dispute resolution dashboard shows all flagged damage reports with photos, timestamps, and locations. Export the entire inspection record (pre-trip photos, post-trip photos, damage annotations, notes, geotagged locations) for insurance claims or customer disputes—all the evidence needed to win a dispute instantly. The mobile interface is optimised for one-handed operation in the field. Photos auto-upload when connectivity is available, but the app works offline too, syncing data later when the driver has a connection. Checklist templates are customisable by vehicle type if different vehicles need different inspection items.
