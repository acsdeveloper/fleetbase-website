## How It Works

1. **Create or import trips** — Build individual trips or bulk-load from your load board (Amazon Relay, TripDash). Define pickup, drop-off, time windows, and cargo requirements in seconds.

2. **Let the system assign intelligently** — AI-powered allocation considers driver location, vehicle fit, hours remaining, and compliance rules. Auto-assign to the best match or manually override if needed.

3. **Dispatch and monitor** — Send trip to driver. Real-time GPS updates stream back to your dashboard. Update customer with live ETA.

4. **Close and report** — Driver marks trip complete. Automatically log timestamps, distance, and cost. Export data for invoicing and analysis.

## Visual Overview

The trip management interface puts control at your fingertips in a layout designed for speed. The main screen shows your trip queue on the left: incoming trips displayed with status (unassigned, assigned, dispatched, in-transit, completed), filterable by date, location, priority, or customer. An unassigned trips panel prominently highlights trips waiting for allocation—click any one to see full details: pickup address, drop-off, time window, vehicle requirements, special instructions. A driver availability sidebar runs down the right, showing a real-time list of available drivers with their location, assigned vehicle, hours remaining, and current trip count. You can drag and drop a trip onto a driver to assign it, or use auto-assign to let the system intelligently match the best driver to each trip.

A map view sits in the centre, showing all active trips and driver locations in real-time. Click any vehicle on the map to see its trip details and live ETA updates. Beneath that is a trip detail card that opens when you select any trip—it shows the full audit trail: creation time, assignment time, dispatch time, driver GPS track, delivery proof (photo, signature, timestamp), and more. You can share this entire record with a customer or export it for dispute resolution.

For bulk operations, a bulk import wizard lets you upload a CSV or connect directly to Amazon Relay. Map your columns once (pickup address → location, time window → delivery window), and the system remembers the mapping for future imports. You can import 100+ trips in seconds. On the mobile app, drivers see their assigned trips in a clean list, tap any trip to see turn-by-turn navigation, and capture proof-of-delivery with one click (photo and signature automatically timestamped).
