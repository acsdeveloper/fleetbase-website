## How It Works

1. **Add items to inventory** — Technician or manager adds parts and equipment. Set quantity, cost, and reorder threshold.

2. **Track usage** — Technician uses part for repair. Logs usage with vehicle ID and repair type. Inventory decreases automatically.

3. **Monitor levels** — System alerts when stock drops below reorder threshold. Manager orders before running out.

4. **Analyse and forecast** — Dashboard shows usage trends. Forecast annual spend and budget accordingly.

## Core Capabilities

- **Inventory registry** — Centralised database of all parts and equipment. Name, description, part number, cost, supplier information.

- **Multi-depot tracking** — Inventory tracked separately per depot. Transfer items between depots. Visibility across entire network.

- **Usage logging** — Technician or manager logs when item is used. Links to vehicle and repair. Tracks full usage history.

- **Reorder threshold management** — Set minimum stock level per item. System alerts when falling below threshold. Manager orders before shortage.

- **Bulk usage requests** — Technician requests multiple items for planned repair. Manager approves. Automatic stock deduction.

- **Supplier integration** — Link items to suppliers. One-click ordering when stock runs low. Automatic delivery scheduling.

- **Cost tracking and forecasting** — Track cost per item and total usage spend. Forecast annual costs. Support budget forecasting.

- **Compliance equipment checklist** — Required safety equipment (vests, triangles, breathalysers) tracked. Linked to vehicle inspection checklist.

## Visual Overview

Inventory management transforms spare parts from a mystery to a managed asset with complete visibility and control. The inventory dashboard sits at the top with KPI cards showing total inventory value, number of items below threshold, stock-outs this month, and excess inventory that's tying up cash. Real-time stock level trends show how your inventory is moving. Below that is a parts inventory grid listing all parts with current stock level, reorder threshold, reorder quantity, and associated supplier. Colour-coding is instant: green means adequate stock, yellow means running low, red means out of stock. Sort by stock level, category, or supplier to find what you need.

Click any part to open its detail card, showing stock level broken down by depot, complete usage history (when used, on which vehicle, by which technician), supplier and cost information, and reorder settings. Photos of the part are included so you know exactly what part is being tracked. Stock level alerts work automatically: a red alert fires when stock falls below threshold, a yellow warning when approaching threshold, and the system triggers a purchase order automatically to your approved supplier. When a technician installs a part during vehicle repair, they log usage directly in the system (which part, which vehicle, date, technician name), and the cost is automatically allocated to that vehicle's maintenance record.

When stock falls below threshold, the system generates a purchase order and sends it to the supplier, showing reorder quantity, delivery address, and required delivery date. It can be approved manually or triggered automatically. If one depot is low on a part but another depot has excess, a technician can request a transfer. The manager approves if it maintains adequate stock at both locations, and levels adjust automatically. A supplier management section lists all approved suppliers for each part type, with contact info, lead times, and pricing tracked. The system can select the best supplier automatically based on cost and delivery time. On mobile, technicians search the parts inventory, log usage, and submit stock requests from the workshop or roadside, and the app works offline for areas with poor connectivity, syncing when a connection is available.
