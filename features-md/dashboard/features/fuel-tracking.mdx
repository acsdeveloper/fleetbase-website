## How It Works

1. **Log fuel purchases** — Enter manually, upload from Excel, or photograph receipt. System reads receipt details via OCR.

2. **Link to vehicles and trips** — Assign fuel purchase to specific vehicle or trip. Automatic date matching with trip records.

3. **Track and analyse** — Dashboard shows fuel consumption per vehicle, cost per litre, and anomalies. Alerts flag unusual activity.

4. **Export and reconcile** — Download fuel data for accounting. Automatic export to accounting systems. Match against fuel card statements.

## Visual Overview

Fuel tracking transforms fuel management from guesswork to data-driven control. The fuel dashboard sits at the top with KPI cards showing your fuel spend this month, average cost per litre across the fleet, cost per mile for the fleet, and number of anomalies detected this week. Charts below show week-over-week and year-over-year trends so you spot cost spikes quickly. Below that is a fuel transaction log showing every fuel purchase: date, vehicle, driver, amount (in litres and cost), cost per litre, location (mapped on a small inline map), and a thumbnail of the receipt image. Filter the log by vehicle, date, location, or anomaly flag to drill into specific patterns.

Anomaly alerts sit in a dedicated section, flagging unusual patterns that might indicate fraud or mechanical issues: fuel purchased twice in one day, cost per litre significantly higher than your usual suppliers, purchase location in an unexpected area, or volume filled unusually high (possible tank overfill). Click any anomaly to investigate—see the receipt image, the driver's other recent purchases, and the vehicle's consumption history. A receipt OCR view shows receipt images automatically analysed by machine learning. Extracted data (date, litres, cost, pump location) is auto-populated into the system, and you see the receipt image alongside the extracted data so you can verify accuracy.

Vehicle fuel trends let you click any vehicle and see its fuel consumption over time, compared against similar vehicles in your fleet to identify outliers that might indicate mechanical problems. Driver fuel behaviour analysis shows which drivers' vehicles consume more fuel—identifying drivers with aggressive driving habits (higher fuel consumption). Cost analytics break down fuel expenses by vehicle, driver, location, or trip, so you can identify specific opportunities for improvement (switch to cheaper pumps, optimise routes, prioritise maintenance). On mobile, drivers photograph receipts and upload immediately during refuelling, so no receipts get lost and data is captured in real-time.
