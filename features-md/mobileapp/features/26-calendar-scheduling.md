# Calendar & Scheduling Integration
## Your schedule at a glance—visual calendar makes planning intuitive and conflict-proof

Scheduling should be visual and intuitive. Spreadsheets and lists are terrible for understanding your actual schedule. Our calendar integration gives you a visual overview: see your entire month at a glance, spot free days instantly, request time off by selecting dates, and avoid double-bookings at a glance. Scheduling becomes easy instead of confusing.

---

## Key Benefits

- **Visual Calendar Display**: See your entire schedule at a glance with colour-coded events and clear date highlighting
- **Intuitive Date Selection**: Interactive calendar for selecting dates without complex navigation
- **Multiple View Options**: Switch between month, week, and day views depending on planning timeframe
- **Appointment Overview**: See all scheduled shifts and events with times, locations, and assignments
- **Easy Leave Requests**: Select dates for time off with calendar context showing existing commitments
- **Conflict Prevention**: Visual display prevents accidental double-bookings or conflicting requests
- **Recurring Events**: Support for repeating shifts, training, and recurring appointments
- **Export Capability**: Export your calendar to external calendar applications for personal planning

---

## How It Works

**1. Open Calendar**
Driver taps the schedule or calendar section to see their calendar overview.

**2. View Schedule**
The calendar displays all assigned shifts with colour coding (regular shifts, special assignments, blocked leave).

**3. Request Time Off**
Driver selects a date range on the calendar and submits a leave request with reason and optional notes.

**4. Track Status**
The calendar updates to show requested leave as pending, approved, or declined with visual indicators.

## Why Choose This Feature

Calendar interfaces seem basic, but they're genuinely valuable for scheduling. A date field where you type "2026-05-20" is error-prone and unintuitive. A calendar where you see the month laid out and click the date is immediately obvious and prevents mistakes.

Visual calendar display prevents the most common scheduling problem—double-booking. When you can see all your existing commitments while selecting dates for leave, you avoid requesting time off you've already been scheduled to work.

The ability to see surrounding days and their status helps with planning. "I want time off June 1-5" is easier to decide when you can see that June 1 is already after your shift ends on May 31, effectively giving you extra time off without using leave.

---

## Visual Overview

The calendar displays the current month in a grid with weekday headers. Days with events show the event count and category colour. Tapping a date shows that day's events below the calendar. To request leave, the driver clicks a date, then holds/drags to select a date range, which highlights in a contrasting colour. A form appears to confirm the request with reason and notes. Approved leave shows with a green background; pending shows with yellow; declined shows with red.
