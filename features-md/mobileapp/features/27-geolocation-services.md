## How It Works

**1. Enable Tracking**
Driver enables location services when starting their shift. Permission requests are handled transparently.

**2. Continuous Monitoring**
The system tracks the driver's location continuously, adjusting sample frequency based on speed and context.

**3. Automatic Detection**
Geofencing automatically recognises when the driver arrives within a delivery zone (typically 50-100 metres of destination).

**4. Log Complete Route**
The complete route is logged with timestamps, creating an immutable record of everywhere the driver went and when.

## Why Choose This Feature

GPS tracking serves multiple purposes. For customers, it means they can track deliveries in real-time and prepare for arrival. For your company, it means visibility into where work is happening and evidence of completed deliveries.

Battery optimisation is the key innovation here. Naive GPS tracking drains batteries rapidly. Intelligent sampling solves this. When the driver is moving 60km/h on the highway, location updates can be infrequent. When they're stopped at a delivery location, updates can be more frequent without wasting power because the vehicle isn't moving anyway.

Geofencing automates routine actions. When a driver arrives at a delivery location, the system knows automatically. Instead of manually confirming arrival, an automated proof-of-delivery prompt appears. Routine tasks handled automatically free drivers to focus on customer interaction.

## Visual Overview

On the map, the driver's current position shows as a blue dot. The location accuracy is shown as a circle around the position. As the driver moves, a blue line traces the route. When approaching a delivery location, a geofence zone appears (usually invisible unless in debug mode). Upon arrival within the zone, an alert or notification confirms arrival. The complete route is visible as a continuous blue line with timestamps at significant points.
