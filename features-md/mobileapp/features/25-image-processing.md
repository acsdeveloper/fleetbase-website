# Image Processing & Camera Integration
## Professional photos, optimised automatically—capture, process, and upload in seconds

Photos are crucial for proof of delivery, documentation, and documentation. But processing them is usually painful: slow uploads, quality issues, metadata missing, file management nightmare. Professional image processing eliminates all of this. Your phone's camera captures, processing optimises automatically, metadata extracts instantly, upload happens seamlessly. Photos are never a bottleneck again.

---

## Key Benefits

- **Native Camera Access**: Use device camera with full control over quality, format, and capture options
- **Automatic Optimisation**: Images are automatically resized and compressed for efficient storage and transmission
- **EXIF Metadata**: Location, timestamp, and orientation data automatically captured with every photo
- **Gallery Import**: Select existing photos from device gallery when camera capture isn't available
- **Fast Processing**: Image optimisation happens in the background without blocking the user
- **Memory Efficient**: Intelligent image caching ensures the app remains responsive even with many photos
- **Batch Operations**: Process and upload multiple images efficiently in sequence
- **Quality Assurance**: Automatic image quality validation ensures usable photos before submission

---

## How It Works

**1. Open Camera**
Driver taps the camera icon to capture a delivery photo or document image.

**2. Take Photo**
The device camera opens. Driver frames the shot and taps to capture.

**3. Automatic Processing**
The image is automatically optimised: resized to 1920×1920px maximum, compressed for efficient transmission, and EXIF metadata extracted.

**4. Ready to Upload**
The processed image is ready for immediate upload or can be saved to the device for later submission.

## Why Choose This Feature

Image processing seems simple—take a photo, upload it. But the details matter enormously. Unoptimised images are huge (5-10MB), slow to upload, and blow through data plans. Automated processing reduces them to 200-400KB without visible quality loss.

EXIF metadata is the difference between a photo that proves something and a photo that's just evidence. A delivery photo with GPS coordinates and timestamp that matches the order assignment time is nearly irrefutable proof. A photo without that metadata is just a picture.

Handling both camera and gallery import is important. Sometimes drivers need to use existing photos. Supporting both paths makes the feature flexible without requiring multiple workflows.
